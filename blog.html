Five Simple Ways to Make Your Bedroom Feel Like a Luxury Hotel

Ever entered a luxury hotel and wished your bedroom felt the same? The good news is you do not need to have a five-star budget to have that vibe at your home.

Begin with Luxurious Bedding:
White cotton sheet sets are a must in hotels, with a high thread count. Bring the class in by throwing in a fluffy duvet and a cute bedspread.

Layering Is the Way to Go:
Hotels layer. They use pillows and throw covers to create depth. Put in 2 to 4 pillows and toss on a throw blanket.

Stick with Something Neutral and Calm:
Soft whites, greys, and beiges provide that cool spa feel to a room.

Make It Smell So Good:
Lavender mist or cotton-scented candles will surely make this a little paradise.

Declutter and Organize:
Tidy spaces contribute to good sleep. Use any kind of storage and trays for easier tidying.

Understanding the Essentials Bedding Components

Before diving into the details, let's clarify the key components of your bedding:

Sheets:
These are the foundation, the layers that lie directly against your skin. A complete sheet set typically includes a fitted sheet (with elasticized edges to stay securely on your mattress), a flat sheet (placed over the fitted sheet), and pillowcases.

Duvet:
A duvet is a soft, flat bag filled with down, feathers, wool, silk, or synthetic materials. It acts as your primary source of warmth and comfort.

Bedspread:
A bedspread is a decorative covering that typically extends to the floor, covering the entire bed, including pillows. It can be quilted, woven, or have other decorative finishes and often provides a lighter layer of warmth compared to a duvet.

Pillows:
While not technically bedding covers, pillows are essential for sleep comfort and contribute significantly to the overall aesthetic. We'll touch on decorative pillows later.

The primary purpose of bedding is to provide a comfortable and healthy sleep environment. Consider these factors:

Bedspread weight and textures

Bedspreads offer an additional layer of style and can provide varying degrees of warmth.

Lightweight Bedspreads: Ideal for warmer months or as an extra decorative layer over a duvet. These can be made from cotton, linen, or lightweight synthetics.

Quilted Bedspreads: Feature stitched patterns with a filling, providing a bit more warmth and texture.

Woven Bedspreads: Offer intricate patterns and can range in weight depending on the weave and material.
Consider the overall climate and whether you intend to use the bedspread as your primary source of warmth or as a decorative accent.

Caring for Your Lustique Bedding

Proper care will ensure the longevity and beauty of your Lustique bedding. Always refer to the care labels on your specific items. However, here are some general guidelines:

Duvets: Down and feather duvets may require professional cleaning. Synthetic duvets can often be machine washed on a gentle cycle. Ensure they are thoroughly dried to prevent mildew.
